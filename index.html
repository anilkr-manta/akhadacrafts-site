Akhada Crafts focus

What visitors should understand quickly

Indian clubs are often used for mobility, rhythm, coordination, and shoulder control. Mugdars and heavier traditional tools are commonly presented as strength-oriented implements that emphasize leverage, circular movement, and controlled power.

A good informational site makes that distinction clear, shows the progression path, and gives the visitor a simple next step if they want more detail.

Training structure

A 4-level exercise framework for the site.

This layout is designed around the kind of progression commonly shown in public Indian club and karlakattai content: start with basics, build rhythm, then move toward more complex sequences and heavier training.

Level 1 — Beginner foundation

Recommended for light club work and first exposure to the movement style.

  • Basic grip awareness
  • Front circles
  • Back circles
  • Pendulum swings
  • Shoulder opening patterns

Level 2 — Control and continuity

Used to connect individual drills into a smoother, more controlled practice.

  • Inside circles
  • Outside circles
  • Alternating swings
  • Cross-body patterns
  • Rhythm building transitions

Level 3 — Flow progression

For more developed users who want combinations and continuous movement.

  • Mills
  • Figure-8 variations
  • Double-pattern combinations
  • Reverse-direction transitions
  • Longer flow sequences

Level 4 — Mugdar strength work

For heavier implements and more leverage-driven movement.

  • Offset grip swings
  • Circular strength patterns
  • Back swing variations
  • Shoulder and grip endurance sets
  • Controlled power sequences

Practice notes

Use this section for simple positioning rather than medical or coaching claims.

  • Begin with control, not speed
  • Progress in small jumps
  • Emphasize rhythm and posture
  • Use lighter tools for learning
  • Reserve heavier tools for progression

How this fits your kits

Each level maps naturally to a staged kit structure.

  • 0.5 kg pair for Level 1
  • 1 kg pair for Level 2
  • 1.5 kg pair for Level 3
  • 2 kg mugdar pair for Level 4
