What each pair is good for
1.1 lb / 500 g — Wrist, joint & rehab
The entry point. Used for pendulum swings, wrist circles, and light arm casts. Anyone coming back from a shoulder, elbow, or wrist injury should start here and stay for several weeks. Also excellent for desk workers to counteract postural tightening.
1.8 lb / 800 g — Shoulder mobility
The classic weight for traditional British military patterns — mills, figure-eights, and extended arm circles. Builds full rotator cuff stability and restores complete shoulder range of motion. Best for daily warm-up routines.
3.3 lb / 1.5 kg — Strength endurance
Where training becomes genuinely demanding. The extended lever arm and 3.3 lb weight create far more shoulder load than the same weight in a dumbbell. Use for flows, sport-specific rotational work, and high-rep swinging patterns.
4.4 lb / 2 kg — Full conditioning
The top of the beginner kit range. Grip, forearm, shoulder, and core all engaged at once. Use for low-rep strength sets and finishers after heavier barbell or grappling work.

Why train with these tools

What modern training misses

Indian clubs, mugdar, and samtola address movement patterns and joint angles that barbells, dumbbells, and machines simply cannot reach.

01
Full shoulder range of motion
Most pressing and pulling exercises work the shoulder through a narrow arc. Club swinging uses the full 360° range of the shoulder joint — the way it was designed to move — building strength and flexibility at the same time.
02
Rotator cuff health & prehab
The stabiliser muscles of the rotator cuff are chronically undertrained in gym environments. Light club work (500 g–800 g) directly targets these muscles with low impact, making it one of the most effective shoulder injury prevention tools available.
03
Rotational strength & grip
Heavy clubs and the samtola build rotational strength, grip endurance, and core stability in a way that straight-line barbell work cannot. The lever arm forces the entire chain — fingers to trunk — to work together.
